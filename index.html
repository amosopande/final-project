INTRODUCTION

Journey through Kenyan Tribal foods and learn about the unique practises of Kenya's ethnic groups. Discover staple foods, special occasion meals, cooking styles and more.

TRIBES

1. Kalenjin

The Kalenjins are part of Highland Nilotes sub-group of the Nilotic ethinic group. They are known Highland Nilotes because they settled at the highlands in the Rift Valley region of Kenya. They practice pastoralism known by owning large herds of cattle. They are known to be good athletes especially longdistance running such as Eliud Kipchoge, the marathon world record holder. Their staple food is milk and meat. They are known for their unique "Mursik" which is fermented milk mixed with coal of burnt herbal plants. It is stored in a specially made calabash gourd locally known as sotet. Mursik can be drunk as a beverage or with a combination of Ugali and other foods.

2. Kisii

The Kisiis are part of the Bantus ethinic group. They occupy the Kisii region of Kenya. The region has fertile soil enabling them to be industrious farmers mostly food crops. Kisiis are known for their bananas and a type of fermented milk. They enjoy bananas in different forms like "Matoke" which is flavored cooked peeled green bananas served with soup of stew. The different types of bananas are also rippen to be eaten as fruit or with other foods. The fermented milk is mostly served with Ugali or can be taken as a beverage.

3. Kikuyu

Kikuyu are the largest ethnic group in Kenya. They occupy central highlands of Kenya including Mount Kenya region. The Kikuyus are generally known to be entrepeneurs and businessmen. They are part of the Bantu community and practice mixed farming of crops and livestock mostly cash crops such as coffee and tea. They are known for their cultural food known as "Mukimo/Mokimo" which is a mashed mixture of corn, peas, potatoes and sometimes vegetables. It is served with meat or stew.
