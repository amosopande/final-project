INTRODUCTION

Journey through Kenyan Tribal foods and learn about the unique practises of Kenya's ethnic groups. Discover staple foods, special occasion meals, cooking styles and more. Generally Kenya,s popular staple food is Ugali served with a variety of soups or stews made from vegetables, beans, meat or fish.

TRIBES

There are 47 major tribes in Kenya. We will explore some of their unique foods and cultures.

1. Kalenjin

The Kalenjins are part of Highland Nilotes sub-group of the Nilotic ethinic group. They are known Highland Nilotes because they settled at the highlands in the Rift Valley region of Kenya. They practice pastoralism known by owning large herds of cattle. They are known to be good athletes especially longdistance running such as Eliud Kipchoge (the marathon world record holder). Their regular food consumption is meat and milk from the practise of pastoralism. They are known for their unique "Mursik" which is fermented milk mixed with coal of burnt herbal plants. It is stored in a specially made calabash gourd locally known as sotet. Mursik can be drunk as a beverage or with a combination of Ugali and other foods.

2. Kisii

The Kisiis are part of the Bantus ethinic group. They occupy the Kisii region of Kenya. The region has fertile soil enabling them to be industrious farmers mostly food crops. Kisiis are known for their bananas and a type of fermented milk. They enjoy bananas in different forms like "Matoke" which is flavored cooked peeled green bananas served with soup of stew. The different types of bananas are also rippen to be eaten as fruit or with other foods. The fermented milk is mostly served with Ugali or can be taken as a beverage.

3. Kikuyu

Kikuyu are part of Bantus ethinic group. Kikuyu are the largest ethnic group in Kenya. They occupy central highlands of Kenya including Mount Kenya region. The Kikuyus are generally known to be entrepeneurs and businessmen. Some of the known Kikuyus and their contributions include James Mwangi who founded Equity Bank(The largets bank in East and Central Africa by customer base), Peter Munga who founded Safaricom (The largest telecommunication company in East Africa.) They are part of the Bantu community and practice mixed farming of crops and livestock mostly cash crops such as coffee and tea. They are known for their cultural food known as "Mukimo/Mokimo" which is a mashed mixture of corn, peas, potatoes and sometimes vegetables. It is usually served with meat or stew.

4. Luo

Luo are part of the river lake nilotes. They are located in Nyanza region near Lake Victoria in Kenya. They are generally known to be boastful in their success in academic pursuits to be successful Engineers, Doctors and Lawyers. Some of the known Luos include Barack Obama, Lupita Nyong'o. They are known for their fishing culture and practices. They are known for their choice of food which is fish and "Omena" which is a type of tiny fish. Fish is prepared in different ways like fried, smoked or grilled and is usually served with "Ugali" (which is made from corn floor) or "Sukuma wiki" (which is collard greens).
